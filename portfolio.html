Mean Reversion OU Trading Bot

Objective: Develop an automated trading bot that identifies and exploits mean-reversion opportunities in financial markets using the Ornstein-Uhlenbeck (OU) process.

Methodology: The bot continuously ingests real-time price data, estimates OU process parameters, and generates trading signals when prices deviate significantly from the modeled mean. Position sizing adapts to the strength of the signal, and all trades are simulated and visualized for performance review. The entire pipeline is containerized for robust, 24/7 operation.

Results: The strategy consistently delivered positive results in backtesting, demonstrating robust performance across multiple assets. Achieved a Sharpe ratio of 0.94, indicating strong risk-adjusted returns.

Challenges: Key challenges included maintaining estimator robustness during market regime shifts, ensuring fault tolerance for continuous operation, and balancing execution speed with statistical confidence.

Technologies Used:

  • Pandas
  • NumPy
  • SciPy
  • Matplotlib
  • yfinance
  • Docker

Moving Average Trading Bot

Objective: To develop an automated trading bot based on the moving average crossover strategy.

Methodology: The bot continuously monitors the price of a given asset and executes trades when the short-term moving average crosses the long-term moving average. It uses the Backtrader library for backtesting and live trading.

Results: The trading bot has been deployed and is operating profitably. It has demonstrated the effectiveness of the moving average strategy in certain market conditions.

Challenges: The main challenge was to implement a reliable and fault-tolerant system that can run 24/7 without manual intervention.

ETL Automation Pipeline

Objective: Convert raw, high-frequency market data into fully automated, trustworthy datasets for trading and analytics.

Methodology: Ingest, cleanse, and load streaming data with Python + Pandas into PostgreSQL/TimescaleDB, applying record-level validation that assigns quality scores and triggers real-time alerts.

Results: Reduced manual data-prep time by 90 %, eliminated downstream schema errors, and boosted model stability and back-test accuracy.

Challenges: Balancing millisecond-level latency with fault tolerance, and designing flexible validation rules that evolve with new asset classes.

Rapid Deployment: Containerized with Docker and modular scripts; can be rolled out in minutes, on-prem or in the cloud.
